Movies about Ancient India

Ever since I saw a Tamil (dubbed in Hindi) epic film called Bahubali (1 and 2), I’ve been fascinated by stories and fantasies about ancient India. Most Hindi films are filled with over-dramatic love stories about a young guy meeting a young girl and all the shenanigans that go on until they get together...these movies are basically no different from any American teenage love film; except they include lots of singing and dancing.

Other Hindi movies - the more serious ones seem to rely on activating your emotions with some sort of David vs Goliath theme about overcoming the British empire. I’ve seen dozens of these, and they really don’t provide a lot of entertainment or intellectual stimulation anymore. Bahubali does have a love story attached, but it’s not central to the film. The main theme of Bahubali (1 and 2) is redemption and justice.

Bahubali digital post

Similarly, Mohenjo Daro is a film I just watched tonight on Netflix. It’s about a very real ancient Indus valley city called Mohenjo Daro, which is now in present day Pakistan. Looking at pictures of the city on Google, even today, you can tell just how amazing it was. The movie is a love story (this is India afterall), but again relies on a central theme of a farmer who seeks adventure and eventually redemption. It’s one of the better movies I’ve seen about ancient India, and the colorful costumes during some of the fighting and dance scenes are uniquely fun.

Main characters of Mohenjo Daro

Films about ancient India seem to provide me some sort of other-worldly enjoyment. I rarely get to imagine what India may have looked like when it was filled with military fortresses, elephant-armies, gold-laden palaces, princes with cool and bizarre looking weapons, and princesses in beautiful flowing golden sarees. While I know some of the theatrical costumes in the movies about ancient India are just for effect - still, a lot of the films shoot in real palaces in the northern regions of India, and it’s amazing to see what they may have looked like during India’s golden ages. Chinese cinema does a much better job of highlighting the eccentricities and excitement of their ancient civilizations - it’d be great to see more Hindi films do the same by leveraging the beautiful history of ancient India to connect present day Indian culture to the various kingdoms that ruled before the Mughal empire.

Paadmavaat is another Hindi epic film, and has some of the most beautiful scenes of ancient India I’ve ever seen on film.

Today, with India now becoming a global powerhouse (with high-tech leading the way) - it’s not crazy to imagine the abundant wealth that the country might have in the near future. With a rising middle class - shopping malls and startups popping up all over the place - the country is hopefully in the early-stages of experiencing an economic renaissance. Having been born there, with parents and grandparents who struggled mightily to help bring the country out of dire poverty, it’s astonishing to be able to watch as it’s youngest generations bringing India into something that resembles western modernity.

I can’t wait to see what the future holds for India in my lifetime, and hopefully will get to see some more epic Hindi films about its ancient and colorful past as well.
